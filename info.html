Hover Power Calculator [X]

This calculator is for personal use and planning purposes only.

Data Sources:
1. AFM EU02X503A Rev 11. Page 4-74 (valid until 21 March 2023)
(Torque Required to Hover, 80ft Wheel height, 0% thrust margin)
2. CH149 SMM Annex B - Engine Performance Data/Brief
(Torque Available - Anti-Ice Off)

Altimeter Setting: get this from the nearest station. Can be entered without decimal (2992).

True Altitude: Long click your target in Foreflight; a reasonably accurate height is given after the lat/long under 'Location', on the right.

Pressure Altitude: The calculator uses the rule of thumb ((29.92 - altimeter setting) x 1000) + True altitude. This is not the most accurate method but generally works fine. If you want to use a pressure altitude found through other means, set the altimeter setting to 29.92, then input your own pressure altitude under 'True Altitude'.

Temperature: use the 'Windy' app to get the most accurate temperature. Once you've found your power numbers, check the numbers again using 5 degrees colder or warmer to give you an idea of the best/worst case on scene.

Wind: use 'Windy' and use the same method as above. Note that the the wind charts with Anti-Ice on vs. off are slightly different. The calculator has a different wind table for each anti-ice state.

Aircraft zero fuel weight: this only impacts the AUW. If it's inaccurate, correct it with 'Load'. It should represent the helicopter's AUW minus fuel. It includes the normal SAR Crew and their equipment.

Load: use this to adjust the AUW if you take anything extra (people or equipment) or if you take anything off (sea strip, etc).

Fuel: this also only affects AUW.

AUW: This is the only variable in this section that is used for performance calculations. The AUW influences Power Required (not Power Available).

Power Available: This is calculated from the chart the FE normally uses. It uses interpolation which should be at least as accurate as what the FE provides.

The value for Anti-Ice On is derived from Anti-Ice Off Power Available -8 (as per the SMM). The formula is then applied to find the OEI equivalent: PA * 3 / 2.
Note: this is a maximum (2.5 minute) chart.
Note: the AFM has charts (max. 30 minute) for Power Avaialble, but the calculator uses the FE's chart (from the SMM) to avoid discrepancy.

Power Required: This is calculated from AFM EU02X503A Rev 11. Page 4-74 (valid until 21 March 2023)
'Torque Required to Hover, 80ft Wheel height, 0% thrust margin'
Note: The calculator has a table for each chart: anti-ice on and anti-ice off.
Note: The state of ECS does not affect Power Required to hover.
This chart uses Density Altitude, AUW, and Wind to show an estimate of the torque required to keep the helicopter in the hover out of ground effect.
Density altitude is derived from the Pressure Altitude you provided, and the Temperature, using an ISA (International Standard Atmosphere) table.
The Wind you provide is plotted against a table derived from the charts (anti-ice on or off) to adjust the Power Required.
